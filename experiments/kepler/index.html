Kepler's Laws of Planetary Motion

1st Law: Orbits are ellipses with the central body at one focus
2nd Law: Equal areas are swept in equal times
3rd Law: T² ∝ a³ (period squared is proportional to semi-major axis cubed)

Constraint Theory Perspective

Kepler orbits emerge from the geometric constraint that gravitational force always points toward the focus, creating a deterministic relationship between position, velocity, and acceleration.
